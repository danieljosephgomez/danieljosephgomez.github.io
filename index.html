Dan Gomez

I am Head of Multiomics Structural Biology and Network Physiology Division at GomeBio. We are building out this division/department in the direction of a functional Neuro-Spatial Multi-Omics Structural Biology (NSMOSB) unit. I am also a Molecular and ML Research Scientist and Biomedical AI Engineer at Gomera Research in the Bay Area, where I work on quantum computational immunology and machine learning. I am doing my first masters at Cal State East Bay (CSUEB) in Biological Sciences with a concentration in Virophysics and Structural Molecular Biology. You can read more about this in my About page. I'm planning on doing a second masters in Molecular Science and Software Engineering (MSSE) at UC Berkeley or a MS in Neuroimaging and Informatics (NIIN) at USC. In the future, I will be working to complete a PhD in Physical Biology, Bioinformatics: Biological and Medical Informatics (BMI), or Structural, Computational Biology and Neurophysics/Biophysics.

My undergraduate school research at JABSOM, UH Mānoa, was with Dr. Bruce Shiramizu and Vivek Nerurkar who were also my professors in Neurovirology. I designed experiments that were funded by university grants (UROP) to examine the proteomics (by Luminex) of the CSF of HAND patients and the transcriptomics (RNA ISH experiments) of necropsy brain tissue for a proof-of-concept study. After doing functional immunoassays, I volunteered in Hemal Patel's Cardiac & Neuro Protection Laboratories at the UCSD School of Medicine in the VA Healthcare Hospital. I won an award to join the DiVERGE Program Cohort 2020, which allowed me to arrive at UF and join Dr. Chris Vulpe's Toxicology Lab in the Deaprtment of Physiological Sciences. I was able to assist in research with a PhD candidate who had projects in high-throughput toxicity models, mitotoxicity (Mitotox), and metabolomics. At this point, I worked with I was able to complete my undergraduate classes in biology. Towards the end of my degree I was hired by Dr. Xin Chen in the Department of Bioengineering and Therapeutic Sciences at UCSF to move her lab to the University of Hawaii Cancer Center. I began to gain a deep appreciation for the impact that cancer researchers are making in the community locally and globally. Since my first mentor was a Pediatric Oncologist, I always circled back to cancer research. In fact, my first publication's main focus was combining my intial expertise neurovirology and oncogenic viruses. This co-infection and cancer publication has been well received by the scientific community as this review paper has been cited four times as of March 2023. One citation was for a PhD student to obtain their doctorate degree in their dissertation. Other articles are building on our research to tell a story about microbes and their impact on cancer. Finishing my degree, I took classes in Cancer Biology, Genetics, Honors Genetics, Biochemistry I & II, General Virology, Physics, and Experiments in Cell and Molecular Biology that prepared me for graduate studies. I received my Bachelor of Science in Molecuar Cell Biology from SFSU, Class of 2022, where I conducted research with Scott Roy and Nicole Salazar. This research with Dr. Salazar was successful and she continues to study chemokines in cancer. Since getting my degree, I instantly aligned with the largest biotechnology company in the world Roche Diagnostics. I was working in Department of Antibody Formulations of Roche Tissue Diagnostics (RTD). I desired to do my own research and joined an active research lab at Drexel University College of Medicine in the Department of Microbiology & Immunology. We focused on dendritic cells (DCs) and then explored neurological infections which I had been researching for a decade at this point. I co-first authored this publication previously mentioned in co-infection and cancer. I continued to write in cancer as I got started in my MS program at CSUEB. I continue to write, take graduate classes, and review and edit research publications in the scientific and medical community. I have been invited to speak and give presentations in conferences around the world.

At GomeBio, I am building out a biotechnology company that will benefit customers ready to discover their story by learning how to teach their brain and immune system to fight disease.
At Gomera where we identify cures, advance diagnostics, and deliver effective therapeutics to attenuate the global death toll from life-threating diseases/conditions. To do this, I am the Head of R&D Department and Translational Medicine, I am expanding a division in Neuro-Spatial Multi-Omics Structural Biology (NSMOSB). I am also overseeing the development of the Gomera App, AI-assistance for robotic surgery, Surgical Data Science, and oversee the direction of the company as a whole.

Daniel J. Gomez
